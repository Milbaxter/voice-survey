## Value Proposition Breakdown

### What They Pay Now (CATI - Phone Interviews)

**Costs:**
- Interviewer labor: €20-30/hour
- Average call: 10-15 minutes
- 200 calls = 33-50 hours of interviewer time
- **Labor cost:** €3,000-4,000
- Project management: €1,000-2,000
- Platform/software: €500
- **Total:** €8,000-10,000

**Problems:**
- People don't answer unknown numbers
- Multiple call attempts needed (wastes time)
- Interrupts people at bad times (dinner, work, etc.)
- Slow (takes weeks to reach 200 people)
- Expensive (human labor)

### What We Offer (Async Voice Surveys)

**Costs:**
- No interviewer labor (self-administered)
- AI transcription: €30
- Hosting: €50
- Participant incentives: €2,000
- Project management: €1,000
- **Total:** €3,080

**Benefits:**
- People respond when convenient (higher quality answers)
- No annoying phone calls
- Faster (no call scheduling, just send link)
- Same voice data quality
- 40-50% cheaper

## Pitch Points for Discovery Calls

**When they say:** "We do phone interviews now"

**You say:**  
> "Perfect, that's exactly what we replace. Same voice data, but people respond on their own time instead of getting interrupted by a call. How many people do you usually interview? [200] And what does that cost you now? [€8-10K] We can do it for €5,999 with the same quality data, just faster and less intrusive."

**When they say:** "People prefer talking to a real person"

**You say:**  
> "I get that. But think about when YOU get those calls - are you ever happy about it? Most people prefer to respond when it's convenient for them, not when a random number calls during dinner. Plus, they can think about their answers instead of being put on the spot."

**When they say:** "What about follow-up questions?"

**You say:**  
> "Good point. For complex surveys where you need back-and-forth, phone interviews are still better. But for most feedback surveys with 3-7 questions where you just want honest opinions, async voice works great. And you can always do a small phone follow-up with the most interesting responses."
